I am **Pan Xuhan**, an Electronic and Information Engineering graduate from **Imperial College London** and a Master's student in Computer Science at the University of Southern California, specializing in artificial intelligence. My work focuses on practical AI systems across large language models, multimodal learning, speech interaction, computer vision, and deployment.

I am interested in engineering work where model behavior has to survive real product constraints: latency, structured outputs, edge devices, streaming services, and user-facing reliability.

## Current Focus

- **LLM applications**: structured generation, prompt and prefix tuning, model evaluation, and AI-assisted product workflows.
- **Multimodal AI**: text, audio, and video modeling for emotion, sarcasm, and interaction understanding.
- **Speech and perception systems**: TTS, OCR, hand tracking, camera streaming, and edge integration.
- **Applied ML engineering**: reproducible training pipelines, deployment, backend integration, and interactive learning tools.

## Recent Work

- Built [AI voice-interaction terminal features](/portfolio/ai-companion-terminal/) using MediaPipe, PaddleOCR, fish-speech TTS, Docker, and ESP32-CAM streaming.
- Developed [backend and AI features at Tencent AI Arena](/portfolio/tencent-ai-arena/), including structured LLM output for text-driven game interface editing.
- Built [multimodal sarcasm recognition pipelines](/portfolio/multimodal-sarcasm/) using MUStARD, Transformer encoders, Whisper, Qwen, Gemini, and GPT-style analysis.
- Compared [real estate demand forecasting](/portfolio/real-estate-demand/) models with lag features, boosted trees, TFT experiments, and weighted ensembles.
- Implemented systems projects including a [BOSS multi-way join engine](/portfolio/boss-join-engine/), [C90 to RISC-V compiler](/portfolio/c90-riscv-compiler/), and [maze-solving balancing robot](/portfolio/maze-solving-robot/).
- Created an interactive browser-based [Machine Learning Visualization hub](/portfolio/ml-visualization/) for linear regression and K-means clustering.
